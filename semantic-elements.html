Semantic-Elements

A Semantic Web allows data to be shared and reused across applications, enterprises, and communities. And simply put, a semantic element clearly describes its meaning to both the browser and developer.

Semanti-Elements are primarily used to enhance:

  • accessibility -help assistive technologies read and interpret your webpage
  • searchability - help computers make sense of your content
  • internationalization - only 13% of the world are English native speakers
  • interoperability - help other programmers understand the structure of your webpage

Examples of non-semantic elements would be "div" and "span". These tags tell nothing about its content.

Examples of semantic elements could be: "form", "table", and "article". These clearly define its content for both the browser and developer.
